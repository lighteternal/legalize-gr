The Deputy Minister of Culture with Responsibilitie for Sport, Andreas Fouras, greeted the participants and chaired the Balkan Conference of Ministers with Responsibilities for Sport. The participants adopted the following agenda: -Modernising the Balkan Games and creating proto?cols to ensure that the Balkan Games meet contem?porary needs -The Balkans on the course toward the Olympic Games in 2004 -New forms of development and cooperation be?tween Balkan countries in the field of social sport -Fair play and contemporary deviations -Scientific support for competitive sport. The Conference agreed to provide mutual technical, scientific and financial support to the less developed Balkan countries. After the discussion of these topics, the following conclusions were approved: «Balkan sports cooperation based on the indepen?2799 of the 2004 Olympic Games, which will be held in Greece, make it necessary to further strengthen the sporting relations between Balkan countries and to upgrade the athletic meetings and competitions held in the Balkans «These competitions, despite the enriched pro?gramme of international sporting events, continue to be of special significance and should be treated by sports federations in a uniform way, taking into ac?count the special features of each sport The sports federations, taking part in the effort to en?sure the continued improvement of Balkan Sports, are called upon to adopt and implement the decisions of the Balkan Conference of Ministers with Responsibili?ties for Sport on 17 January 1999 and of the meeting of sports experts held at the same time.» The calendar of the Balkan games in 1999 was adapted to these decisions and constitutes an integral part of the present document. The Conference ac?cepted the proposal of the Organizing Committee «A?thens 2004» for a Permanent cooperation between the Balkan and the Black Sea countries with a view to information exchange and establishing a creative re?lationship of all parties involved in the organization of the 2004 Olympic Games.
