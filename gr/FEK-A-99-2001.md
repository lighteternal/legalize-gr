ΒΕΡΕΛΗΣ -PROTOCOL of the 7th Session of the Hellenic - Albanian Intergovernmental Joint Committee for Economic, Industrial and Technical - Scientific Cooperation The 7th Session of the Hellenic - Albanian Intergovern?mental Joint Committee for Economic, Industrial and Tech?nical - Scientific Cooperation, was held in Athens, on 25-27 October 1999. The Greek delegation was headed by Mrs. Rodoula Zis?si, Deputy Minister of National Economy of the Hellenic Re?public. The Albanian delegation was headed by Mrs. Ermelinda Meksi, Minister of Economic Cooperation and Trade. The composition of the two official delegations is listed in Annex I to this Protocol. During her stay in Athens Mrs. E. Meksi had meetings with Minister of National Economy, Mr. Yannos Papanto?niou and Minister of Transport and -Telecommunication Mr. A. Mandelis. The works of the Session were held in a cordial and 1817 The two Sides exchanged information on the current economic situation and the development of their national economies, the legal and institutional framework, as well as, the economic policy measures in the two countries, which can facilitate and stimulate the development of the bilateral trade relations and economic cooperation. The two sides agreed on the importance of fostering mutual contacts and reciprocal visits between the repre?sentatives of private and public sectors in the two respec?tive countries, as well as the need of broadening cooper?ation at all levels. Both Sides agreed that the volume of trade, does not rep?resent the real potential of the economies of the two coun?tries. Therefore, both Sides agreed to further encourage bi?lateral contacts in order to achieve more positive results in the expansion of trade and services. The two Sides are interested in developing and diversi?fying trade exchanges taking into account the increased share of the private sector, as well as the importance of the cooperation in the production field. Both sides agreed to make their best efforts towards the facilitation of trade and emphasis was given to the customs rules and procedures to be followed as provided by the E.U. regulations, bilateral agreements and under?takings as described in the cross border facilitation agree?ments within the frame of SECI (South East Cooperation Initiative). The Albanian side expressed its satisfaction to the Hel?lenic side for the soft Loan of GRD 20 billion the Greek Gov?ernment has granted to the Albanian Government, to over?pass the economic situation that was created by the crisis of 1997. From this amount, GRD 2 billion has been offered as a grant by the Greek Government. For the remaining amount which will be offered as a Loan, the relevant Proto?col was signed in Tirana, on October 15, 1997. The amount of GRD 18 billion will be used as follows: • GRD 3 billion, in order to face problems of macroeco?nomic character and more specifically to support the Bal?ance of Payment of Albania • GRD 5 billion for social infrastructure programs • GRD 5 billion for housing programs • GRD 5 billion for private sector support, with priority to those of the Greek. Greek-Albanian and Albanian interests enterprises that suffered damages during the riots of 1997 in Albania, and wish to continue their activity and oper?ations there. Both Sides expressed their satisfaction for the imple?mentation of the first part of the program for support the private sector amounting to 550 mil. GRD. The two sides agreed to proceed to the immediate es?tablishment of the Greek - Albanian Surveillance Com?mittee as it is referred in the loan Agreement of the 5 bil. GRD concerning the Housing Program. The first meeting of this Committee will be held by 20th of November 1999. In addition the parties agreed that the Joint Committee for the Greek loan will meet until the end of the year 1999, to evaluate the propose of the Housing Programmed based on Surveillance Committee Report. They also agreed on the necessity to proceed negoti?ations with an effort that the signing of all relative loan agreements for the implementation of the Social Infras?tructure program, be completed by the end of the year. Also, reference was made to the Albanian debt towards Greece concerning the credit line, which amounts to USD 23,4 million. They decided that the issue will be settled through meetings of representatives from the Greek and Albanian Governments. The outstanding debt to Greek companies concerning Greek products exported in 1990 and 1991 to state owned Albanian companies, have been discussed. Both Sides agreed to organize a meeting during the beginning of November, 1999 between the representatives of the Pan?hellenic Exporters Association and the representatives of the relevant Albanian Authorities n Order to determine the amount of the final overdue debt and to proceeo^to its settlement. The Albanian side has repeatedly confirmed the exis?tence of the debt -balance from the banking arrangemen?ts (Inter- Bank Agreement of 19/11/87) between the former State Bank of Albania and the Bank of Greece. The two . sides agreed on the necessity of a meeting between experts of the two countries for the determination of the fi?nal debt. Regarding the debt of ALBANIA TELECOM to the Hel?lenic Telecom Organization S.A., for the amount of USD 4.743.402,24 the two sides agreed to submit proposals as soon as possible in order to discuss the way of payment debt. As far as the Albanian privatization program is concerned Greek side . pointed out that this program is of great inter?est for Greece. To this end the Greek side asked the Alba?nian to provide continuously the relevant information. Both sides agreed that the improvement of infrastruc?tures (transport, communications, banking e.t.c.) as well as social stability and proper application of laws and bilat?eral agreements regarding the protection of foreign invest?ments will create a positive climate for increase of Greek in?vestments in Albania. The Greek side also asked the Albanian side to eliminate taxes applied to the imports of industrial and other equip?ment which is part of the Greek contribution to various in?vestment projects. The Albanian Side informed that within the framework of the agreements signed for the accession of the Albania in WTO the tariff ceilings reduced from 40% to max 20% and in the medium term they will be reduced again. In the frame of the 7th Interministerial Committee be?tween Greece and Albania, has been proposed by HEPO, Hellexpo and DIPEK from the Greek side to cooperate with respective organizations from Albania in order to develop actions enhancing further the commercial and economic relations between Greek and Albanian businessmen.
